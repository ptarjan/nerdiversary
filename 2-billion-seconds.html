When will you be 2 billion seconds old?

Your second billion seconds takes just as long as the first, but gets none of the press. It arrives about 63 years and 4½ months after you were born — squarely in "still plenty of time to celebrate properly" territory. Enter your birthday to find your exact moment.

The math

  • 2,000,000,000 seconds ÷ 86,400 seconds per day = 23,148 days
  • 23,148 days ÷ 365.2425 days per year ≈ 63.38 years
  • Bonus nerd milestone: at 68.1 years you pass 2³¹ seconds — the number that overflows a 32-bit Unix clock in the year 2038

Almost nobody reaches 3 billion seconds — that takes 95.1 years. If you get there, you have earned the biggest cake technology can produce.
