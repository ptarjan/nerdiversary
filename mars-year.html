How old are you in Mars years?

A year on Mars lasts 687 Earth days — about 1.88 Earth years. Your Mars birthdays are rarer and stranger than the Earth kind, and yes, you are much younger there. Enter your birthday to find your Martian age and your next Mars-year milestone.

The math

  • Mars orbits the Sun every 686.98 Earth days
  • Your age in Mars years = your age in Earth days ÷ 686.98
  • A 30-year-old Earthling is a spry 15.9 in Mars years

The calculator does all seven other planets too. Mercury birthdays come every 88 days; if you make it to one Neptune year (165 Earth years), notify the press.
